About this property

History meets future

The will to change and adapt to the market has been an important driving force behind Mustad's success. It is therefore no coincidence that the building is called Bright House. Fish hook production began in Gjøvik as early as 1877, when Halfdan Torp developed the world's first fully automatic fish hook machine. O. Mustad & Søn became a world leader and led the export of fish hooks to over 160 countries.

In this very building, the hooks were acid treated, polished and made ready for the world market. The process was called “brightning” — hence the name Bright House.

Today, Bright House is a modern commercial building with flexible premises for tenants in various industries. With its unique history and central location, Bright House is a place where the past meets the future — a house for businesses that want to grow and thrive in a dynamic community.

Here you will find offices, a communal canteen and meeting rooms. The meeting rooms can be rented by external parties and are used free of charge by the building's tenants.

HealthINN — The Health Workshop

The Health Workshop at Bright House is a collaboration arena where HealthINN arranges a number of professional desks, meeting places and workshops that facilitate the co-creation, development and testing of new services and technology within health and education.

The health workshop is a flexible space that is well suited for workshops, creative work, courses and seminars.
